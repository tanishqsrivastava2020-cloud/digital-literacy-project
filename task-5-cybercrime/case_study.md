With the rapid expansion of digital payments in India, especially through Unified Payments Interface (UPI), cyber fraud has emerged as a significant concern. UPI-based fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting students and young users due to their frequent use of mobile payment applications.

A widely reported form of fraud involves “social engineering” techniques, where attackers manipulate victims into taking actions that compromise their financial security. In a typical case, a fraudster impersonates a bank representative, customer care executive, or even a known contact. The victim is contacted through phone calls, SMS, or messaging platforms and is informed about an urgent issue such as account suspension, KYC update, or suspicious activity.

The attacker then sends a payment request or malicious link. In UPI fraud cases, victims often receive a “collect request,” which appears similar to receiving money. Due to a lack of awareness, the victim approves the request, unknowingly authorizing a debit transaction. In some cases, users are tricked into installing remote access applications, allowing attackers to control their devices and access sensitive financial data.

According to various reports in India, cyber fraud cases have increased significantly alongside the growth of digital payments. The ease of use of UPI, while beneficial, has also made it easier for scammers to exploit inexperienced users.

The consequences of such fraud include immediate financial loss, psychological stress, and potential misuse of personal data. In extreme cases, repeated fraud attempts may lead to identity theft and long-term financial damage.

This case study demonstrates that cybercrime is not purely technical but heavily relies on human vulnerability. Awareness, vigilance, and informed decision-making are the most effective defenses against such threats.
